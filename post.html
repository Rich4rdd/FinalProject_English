Create an application letter and curriculum vitae for your job listing

What Is a Application Letter?

If you are going to apply any job, usually you need to write an application letter to state that you apply the job vacancy.In the application letter, you need to introduce yourself, mention the position you apply and argue how you will be the best person to fill the position. Along with application letter, you also need to attach some important documents like certificates and others.

An application letter is a document that you send to potential employers to secure a position or get an invitation to an interview. The letter provides the recruiting managers with essential information that describes your skills, passion, experience and educational background.

A. How to write an Application Letter

  • Begin and end the letter appropriately.
  • Give a clear reason for writing.
  • Use linking words to join similar ideas into paragraphs.
  • Include a summary comment near the end of your letter.
  • Close your letter with a set phrase.

    C. Format of Application Letter

    There are so many forms of application letter used in applying the job. The common form used in Indonesia is usually different from ones used in other countries. Therefore, you need to be wise to choose which form to use in writing application letter by knowing your target company. So, find any information of your target company whether it is local, national or international company and its preferences. Then, you choose which form you will use for it.

    What Is Curriculum Vitae

    A Curriculum Vitae (CV) is a document that summarizes a person's education, work history, and achievements. It's often used when applying for academic positions, such as research roles or jobs at universities or medical facilities. The term comes from the Latin phrase curriculum vitae, which literally translates to "course of life.

    Along with the application letter, an applicant usually need to attach the resume or curriculum vitae. There are also so many forms of CV that you can see online, but please choose the most complete, simple and easy to read.

    A. Content Of CV

    • Formal Photograph
    • Personal Data (including your full name, place and date of birth, address, gender, weight and height of body, phone number, email, etc)
    • Education (both formal (schools and universities) and informal (courses) education)
    • Work Experience (list your working experiences)
    • Organization/ Social Activities (if any)
    • Additional Skill/Competences (Your additional skills will be valuable to be chosen to fill the position, especially the certified competences).
